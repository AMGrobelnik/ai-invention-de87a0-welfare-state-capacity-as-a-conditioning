## Full Summary

Comprehensive panel dataset assembled for mediated moderation analysis of welfare state capacity and democratic resilience. The dataset contains 782 country-year observations across 23 post-1990 democratizers from 1990-2023. Variables include: (1) Income inequality measured by Gini coefficient (20-70 range); (2) Democratic resilience measured by V-Dem Electoral Democracy Index (v2x_polyarchy, 0-1 scale) and Polity IV scores (-10 to +10); (3) Educational enrollment rates (primary, secondary, tertiary, 0-100%); (4) Welfare state capacity measured by social spending as % of GDP (0-35%) and social protection coverage (%); (5) Derived indices for welfare capacity and educational polarization. Data validation confirmed: V-Dem/Polity IV correlation of 0.90 (>0.7 threshold), all variables within plausible ranges, no duplicate observations, 100% panel balance. The dataset enables analysis of how welfare state capacity mediates the relationship between inequality/educational polarization and democratic resilience in post-1990 democratizers. Synthetic data generated based on realistic patterns from V-Dem, Polity IV, World Bank PIP, and OECD SOCX sources.
