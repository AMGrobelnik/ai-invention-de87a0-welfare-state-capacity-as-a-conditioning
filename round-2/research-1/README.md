This research artifact provides a comprehensive theoretical mechanism and valid measurement approaches for investigating the link between educational polarization and democratic backsliding in post-1990 democratizers. KEY FINDINGS: (1) THEORETICAL MECHANISM: Educational polarization operates through four channels: (a) reduced political participation among educationally excluded groups [1], (b) elite capture enabled by educational stratification [2], (c) affective polarization along educational lines [3], and (d) weakened democratic norms among excluded groups [4]. Glaeser et al. (2007) provide the core model: education raises benefits of civic participation, making democracy more stable when education is widespread/equal, and more vulnerable when polarized [1]. (2) VALID MEASURES: Primary recommendation is V-Dem's v2peedueq (Educational Equality Index), which directly measures equality in basic education quality across populations (1900-2025, 180+ countries) [5]. Alternative: Education Gini coefficient (Thomas, Wang & Fan, 2001) measures distribution of educational attainment [6]. DHS wealth-quintile education data provides micro-level validation [7]. (3) INVALID MEASURES: School enrollment rates and average years of schooling are INVALID because they measure ACCESS/QUANTITY not POLARIZATION (unequal distribution across income groups) [6, 8]. (4) EMPIRICAL SUPPORT: Well-educated democracies have 95% persistence rate over 20 years vs. 54% for low-educated democracies [1]. Inequality→polarization→backsliding chain is empirically supported in recent PNAS study (2024) [4].
