## Layman Summary

Country-year panel data from OWID covering democracy scores, income inequality, welfare spending, and education inequality for 191 countries from 1990-2020, enabling analysis of how welfare state capacity affects democratic resilience in post-1990 democratizers.

## Full Summary

Panel dataset (N=5,179 country-year observations, 191 countries, 1990-2020) constructed from OWID sources: Polity IV (democracy scores), EIU Democracy Index, WIID (inequality Gini), OECD Social Expenditure Database (welfare capacity), and Clio-Infra (education inequality Gini). Variables include democracy_polity (-10 to +10), regime_polity (0=autocracy, 1=anocracy, 2=democracy), democracy_eiu, inequality_gini, welfare_capacity_gdp_share (%), educ_polarization_gini. Missing data: democracy_polity 1.2%, inequality_gini 56.4%, welfare_capacity 81.1%, education inequality 47.5%. Dataset formatted as regression task predicting democratic resilience from inequality, welfare capacity, and education polarization. Codebook and summary statistics included.
